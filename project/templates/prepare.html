In case you were wondering what this is all about ...

TILE GAME


UWE researchers are testing technology-enhanced sports equipment that could aid users recovering from lower leg injuries. By jumping on sensor-fitted floor tiles in sequences, users can regain strength and stability.

Summary

Preparing for your Tile Game

Double-leg jumping technique:

Stand equally balanced with your feet shoulder-width apart, and make sure your knees don't drop inwards. Lower your body into a half-squat and get your arms ready by your side. Push off from the balls of your feet, swing the arms fowards and up as you extend your hips, knees, and ankles. Land slightly leaning forward, on the balls of your feet. Bend the knees making sure both legs equally support the impact of your landing. Bring the arms back down to your side.

Health first

Please focus on the quality of your movements during the game, as this is key to rehabilitation. Please don't perform any moves your physio or doctor advised you against. Should you feel any pain beyond 'normal' muscle burn, please stop and let us know.

Why this research?

This project hopes to support users who have more specific needs than the average healthy population. By taking part you will help us to gain a better understanding of tech-enhanced exercise devices for users with lower limb rehabilitation needs.

Video recordings

We would love to video record a section of your gameplay to improve the game. The camera can also be set to only show your lower limbs on the tiles or you can ask us not to video-record at all. Please let us know about your preferences in the consent form.

Your data

All information you give will be kept confidential and anonymised at point of data transcription. We will review your anonymised data, but you will not be re-identifyable. We will store all data until a further funding application and paper publications are submitted.

How do the tiles work?

Pressure sensors on the tiles collect how a person performs during the game, and we'd be happy to share your performance data with you afterwards. There is no electricity flowing through your body, or any camera tracking involved.

Any other questions?

If you have questions please feel free to speak to the researchers. You can pause or stop at any time during the game. After gameplay we would like to hear about your experience of the game so we can adapt it further for lower leg rehabilitation.

Further Information

About this Research

TILE GAMES is funded by a UWE Bristol Research, Business and Investment Seed Fund Award. The interdisciplinary team is led by Dr Michaela Palmer. The team’s biographies and details of work are available on the UWE staff profile site.

The aim of this project is to deliver a proof‐of‐concept prototype of an adaptive system for lower leg rehabilitation. By stepping and jumping on sensor-fitted floor tiles in specific sequences users can regain movement and strength in the injured limb. The system is set to perform within safe but individually challenging boundaries and optimize physical gameplay to maximise user rehabilitation and enjoyment.

At the prototype stage, the researchers want to find out about user acceptance of this technology and whether physical games may help keep users motivated to continue to perform rehabilitation exercises. Results will feed into a peer-reviewed academic paper as well as a funding application to develop the project further.
