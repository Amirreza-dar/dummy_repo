About this Research

TILE GAMES is funded by a UWE Bristol Research, Business and Investment Seed Fund Award. The interdisciplinary team is led by Dr Michaela Palmer. The team’s biographies and details of work are available on the UWE staff profile site.

The aim of this project is to deliver a proof‐of‐concept prototype of an adaptive system for lower leg rehabilitation. By stepping and jumping on sensor-fitted floor tiles in specific sequences users can regain movement and strength in the injured limb. The system is set to perform within safe but individually challenging boundaries and optimize physical gameplay to maximise user rehabilitation and enjoyment.

At the prototype stage, the researchers want to find out about user acceptance of this technology and whether physical games may help keep users motivated to continue to perform rehabilitation exercises. Results will feed into a peer-reviewed academic paper as well as a funding application to develop the project further.
